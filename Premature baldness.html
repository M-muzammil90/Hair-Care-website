Understanding Premature Baldness

Early hair loss is often multifactorial. Identifying your specific causes is the first step toward effective treatment.

Genetic Predisposition

Androgenetic alopecia affects up to 50% of men by age 50, often beginning in early 20s with distinct patterns.

Chronic Stress

Elevated cortisol and inflammation can prematurely push hair follicles into resting phase (telogen effluvium).

Nutritional Deficits

Low iron, zinc, vitamin D and protein intake starve follicles of building blocks for healthy growth.

Autoimmune Factors

Conditions like alopecia areata cause patchy loss when immune system attacks hair follicles.

Medication Effects

Certain antidepressants, blood pressure meds, and acne treatments list hair loss as side effect.

Hormonal Imbalances

DHT sensitivity, thyroid disorders, and PCOS create environments hostile to hair retention.
